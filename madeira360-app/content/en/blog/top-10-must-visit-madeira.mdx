# What to See on Madeira: Top 10 Must-Visit Places According to Travelers

**Short Answer (Featured Snippet Optimized)**

The top places to see on Madeira are:
Levada 25 Fontes, Caldeirão Verde, Pico do Arieiro to Pico Ruivo, Cabo Girão Skywalk, Porto Moniz, Monte Palace Tropical Garden, Ponta de São Lourenço, Funchal Old Town, Santana and the Fanal Forest.
These locations showcase Madeira's natural beauty, landscapes and culture and are considered must-see attractions by travelers.

Madeira is often called the "Island of Eternal Spring." Dramatic mountains, scenic hiking trails, cliffs, gardens and charming towns make it one of Europe's most diverse travel destinations.
Below are the 10 places most frequently recommended by travelers, including who they are best suited for, how much time to plan and why they are considered essential.

![Madeira Mountains and Hiking Trails](/images/top-10-sehenswurdigkeiten-madeira.jpg)

## 1. Levada das 25 Fontes

**Best for:**
✔ Nature lovers
✔ Beginners and families with older children

**Time needed:** 3–4 hours

**Why it's a must-see:**
One of Madeira's most famous levada walks, this trail leads through lush forest to a lagoon with 25 waterfalls. Easy access and stunning scenery make it ideal for first-time visitors or guided hikes.

## 2. Caldeirão Verde

**Best for:**
✔ Active travelers
✔ Hiking enthusiasts

**Time needed:** 4–5 hours

**Why it's a must-see:**
This hike includes tunnels, narrow paths and a powerful waterfall. Caldeirão Verde is often considered one of the most impressive nature hikes on the island.

## 3. Pico do Arieiro → Pico Ruivo

**Best for:**
✔ Experienced hikers
✖ Not suitable for beginners or those with fear of heights

**Time needed:** 5–6 hours

**Why it's a must-see:**
This iconic mountain trail connects Madeira's third-highest peak to its highest summit. Expect rugged terrain and unforgettable views above the clouds.

## 4. Cabo Girão Skywalk

**Best for:**
✔ All travelers
✔ Families and short stops

**Time needed:** 30–60 minutes

**Why it's a must-see:**
At 580 meters, Cabo Girão is one of Europe's highest sea cliffs. The glass skywalk offers dramatic views of the Atlantic Ocean and the south coast.

## 5. Porto Moniz Natural Pools

**Best for:**
✔ Families
✔ Relaxed travelers

**Time needed:** 2–3 hours

**Why it's a must-see:**
These volcanic rock pools allow safe swimming in the Atlantic and are one of Madeira's most unique attractions, often included in island tours.

## 6. Monte Palace Tropical Garden

**Best for:**
✔ Couples
✔ Culture and nature lovers

**Time needed:** 2–3 hours

**Why it's a must-see:**
One of Europe's most beautiful gardens, featuring exotic plants, Asian art and panoramic views over Funchal. Easily combined with a cable car ride.

## 7. Ponta de São Lourenço

**Best for:**
✔ Active travelers
✔ Landscape photographers

**Time needed:** 2–4 hours

**Why it's a must-see:**
This eastern peninsula shows a wild, dry side of Madeira. The coastal hike offers dramatic cliffs and sweeping ocean views.

## 8. Funchal Old Town (Zona Velha)

**Best for:**
✔ First-time visitors
✔ Culture seekers

**Time needed:** 2–4 hours

**Why it's a must-see:**
Colorful doors, historic streets and local restaurants make Funchal's old town the cultural heart of Madeira.

## 9. Santana Traditional Houses

**Best for:**
✔ Families
✔ Cultural travelers

**Time needed:** 1–2 hours

**Why it's a must-see:**
These triangular thatched-roof houses are one of Madeira's most recognizable symbols and a popular photo stop.

## 10. Fanal Forest

**Best for:**
✔ Nature lovers
✔ Photographers

**Time needed:** 1–2 hours

**Why it's a must-see:**
A mystical laurel forest with ancient trees, often surrounded by fog. Fanal is part of Madeira's UNESCO World Heritage.

## Conclusion

Whether you enjoy hiking, nature, culture or relaxation, Madeira offers unforgettable experiences for every traveler. These top 10 attractions are the perfect starting point for exploring the island.
